Kasaragod, the northernmost district of Kerala, is a land of enchanting beauty and cultural diversity. Known as the "Land of Seven Languages," it reflects a harmonious blend of traditions and linguistic heritage. Nestled between the Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea, Kasaragod boasts lush landscapes, serene backwaters, and pristine beaches. The district is home to historic forts like Bekal and Chandragiri, along with sacred sites such as the Malik Deenar Mosque and Ananthapura Lake Temple. With its rolling hills, flowing rivers, and vibrant communities, Kasaragod offers a unique tapestry of nature, history, and spirituality, making it a captivating destination for travelers

Top 3 places to visit in Kasaragod

  1. Bekal Fort

    2. Bekal Fort, located in Kasaragod, Kerala, is a majestic coastal fortress that stands as a testament to history and architectural brilliance. Built in the 17th century by Shivappa Nayaka of the Keladi dynasty, it spans over 40 acres and features a unique keyhole-shaped design optimized for defense. The fort's thick laterite walls, bastions, and observation tower offer panoramic views of the Arabian Sea and surrounding landscapes. Within its premises, you'll find intriguing features like a water tank, underground tunnels, and remnants of Tipu Sultan's era. Bekal Fort is not only a historical marvel but also a serene destination, surrounded by pristine beaches and lush greenery, making it a must-visit for history enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

Location: Bekal Fort Rd Nearest Railway Station : Pallikare, or Kanhangad:13km, Kasaragod 671316 India

Entry fee: INR 100 per person

Timings: 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM

  • Ranipuram

    • Ranipuram, often called the "Ooty of Kerala," is a serene hill station nestled in the Kasaragod district. Perched at an altitude of 1,048 meters above sea level, it is a haven for nature lovers and trekkers alike. The landscape is adorned with lush Shola forests, sprawling grasslands, and mist-covered hills that merge seamlessly with the Coorg mountains of Karnataka. Known for its biodiversity, Ranipuram is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including wild elephants, Malabar giant squirrels, and vibrant bird species. The cool breeze, often scented with lemongrass, adds a refreshing touch to the tranquil ambiance. With its picturesque trekking trails and panoramic views, Ranipuram offers an unforgettable escape into nature's embrace.
