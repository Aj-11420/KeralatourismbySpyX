Kollam, a land where nature and life harmoniously unite, radiates a charm that captivates every soul. The tranquil backwaters of Ashtamudi glisten under the sun’s tender embrace, while golden beaches stretch gracefully along the Arabian Sea. Rural landscapes hum with the rhythm of farming life, a serene melody to contrast the vibrant energy of city streets bustling with culture and trade. Here, beauty flows effortlessly between past and present, weaving Kollam’s enchanting tale of nature, heritage, and the spirit of its people.

Top 3 places to visit in Kollam

  1. Jatayu Earth's Centre

    2. Spread across 65 acres of land, Jatayu Earth's Centre is a rock-themed park built to promote mythology and adventure. The world’s largest bird sculpture is the main attraction at Jatayupara Nature Park and is one among the several Kollam famous places. It has a 6D theatre, a digital audio-visual room, cable car and the Siddha Cave Healing Centre. The virtual reality museum here tells the story of Jatayu to visitors. It is also home to umpteen adventure activities including paintball, laser tag, archery, bouldering, rock climbing, air-rifle shooting, zip lining, trekking, rappelling, valley crossing, etc

Location: LMS Vellayambalam Road, Near, Museum Junction, Kanaka Nagar, Nanthancode, Trivandrum, Kerala 695033

Entry fee: INR 20 per person

Timings: 10:00 AM to 4:30 PM, closed on Mondays

  • Thangassery Lighthouse

    • Thangassery Lighthouse rises majestically against the backdrop of Kollam’s serene coastal charm, an enduring sentinel watching over the restless waves. Its radiant beam has guided countless sailors through the mysteries of the Arabian Sea, a beacon of hope and safety in the dark expanse of midnight waters. This iconic structure stands wrapped in layers of history, whispering tales of seafaring adventures and colonial heritage carried by the winds and tides. Its spiral staircase ascends to breathtaking views, where the ocean stretches endlessly, meeting the sky in a seamless embrace. A visit here feels like stepping into a timeless narrative, where nature's grandeur blends effortlessly with human craftsmanship. Thangassery Lighthouse is not just a monument—it’s a storyteller of Kollam’s spirit and an emblem of the deep connection between land, sea, and history.

    Location: Thangassery Lighthouse,KOllam Kerala 695585

    Entry fee: INR 490 average cost per person , free for kids

    Timings: 10:00 AM – 5:00 PM

  • Thirumullavaram Beach

    • Thirumullavaram Beach, situated about 6 km north of Kollam city in Kerala, is a serene coastal retreat known for its brilliant blue waters, golden sands, and tranquil ambiance. This unspoiled beach offers breathtaking views as it stretches into the horizon, making it an ideal spot for relaxation, swimming, and leisurely walks. Surrounded by natural beauty and cultural landmarks, Thirumullavaram Beach is a favorite among both locals and tourists.
    The beach's calm and clear waters make it a perfect destination for swimming, while its serene surroundings provide an inviting atmosphere for picnics. A unique attraction here is the Nyarazhcha Para, or Sunday Rock, a hillock that extends about 1.5 kilometers into the sea and becomes visible from the shore during low tide. This natural feature adds a sense of wonder to the beach's charm.

    Location: Thirumullavaram Beach, Kollam

    Entry fee:Free

    Timings: 6 AM – 5 PM
